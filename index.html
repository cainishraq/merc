How To Use

MTWHF stands for the days of the week to do each exercise on.
If you're on mobile, scroll horizontally on the charts to see the exercises on the right.
Clicking on any of the exercises will show an explanation image.
A x B means A sets of B reps, or do B of the exercise A times. Example: 3x5 means 5 reps, break, 5 reps, break, 5 reps.

Spend a week on each starting progression per exercise (leftmost) then move onto the harder ones progressively (right).
Yes, even if they're "too easy" already.
Building muscle memory with the earlier progressions bulletproofs your form and prevents injury.

Squats [MTWHF]

NOTE: These are by far the most important exercise since legs are the powerhouse of the human body. Your kicks should be several times stronger than a punch and legs are vital to a strong punch too since they are the base/leverage of the kinetic chain. Building leg strength also improves your sprint so they are extremely practical.

Pushups [MWF]

NOTE: These are a classic exercise and rightfully so. They are mechanically superior to a bench press for your shoulders so try benching once you've made good progress with pushups first.
FORM: Do not place your hands too far apart to make it easier; that will destroy your shoulders. Make sure to pull your scapula together and keep a tight back. Lastly, shift your body weight forward a little like you're leaning forward to make the movement shape an arch instead of a diagonal line and lightly pull your hands together while they're planted on the floor.

Fingertip Pushup [TH]

NOTE: Fingertip pushups are almost never mentioned but work the same muscle groups as the dead hang antagonistically (similar to pushups/pullups) so they can be done less frequently to balance each other. Moreover, they can also aid in the prevention of shoulder and back injuries.
FORM: Make sure your knuckles are actively arched outwards, not bent towards your palm. [EXAMPLE]
